Landscape

Oil Pastel | 11"x17" | 2011

I used to really hate oil pastel. I thought it was messy, hard to control, and I could never get the final piece to look exactly how I wanted. This was one of my high school art projects. At the time, I was busy trying to fill out my AP portfolio and didn't want to spend time on an oil pastel drawing that I thought I'd end up hating anyways.

Because of this, I decided I wasn't going to care about making the landscape resemble the photograph. It became more gestural and I ended up "finishing" in about 4 hours. I put it to the side and began working on other pieces.

The next morning, I went to pack up all of my stuff for school, and to my surprise I actually liked how the piece turned out. I decided to spend more time polishing things off; the reflections in the water, adding more trees, etc. I'm really thankful and proud of this piece because it helped me branch out from trying to make everything photorealistic.
